Best and Worst Tickets Explained

As covered in "Avoid False Tips and Make Better Tickets", all possible draws for any lottery have the same likelihood of occuring. Therefore it seems strange that some tickets can be better than others. To explain, we're going to make a fictional lottery called "Pot Luck" and use the LoTI app to do the analysis. As can be seen above, all Worst's ticket rows are the same. This means you have the same chance of winning as a single row ticket. However, if you do win, you win on all 10 lines, seemingly making it just as good as any other 10 row ticket. This is not the full truth as:

  1. You can expect to wait 10 times longer before your first win. For divisions of small odds, this could mean the difference between a life changing win for you, or your distant descendant.
  2. Most lotteries, (particularly for the top divisions,) divide the prizes by the number of winners, so winning 10 times on the same division and draw, returns less than winning once on 10 different draws - significantly less if you are the only 1st division winner.

To make the second point more clear, consider a shared prize division that has jackpotted to one million dollars. The table below calculates the expected prizes for winning 10 times on different draws and 10 times on the same draw, with respect to the number of other winners:

Thus, when prizes are shared, the expected return for wins across multiple draws will never be less than the expected return for same draw multiple wins - often significantly more.

The average coverage of all 10 row tickets for this lottery is 83.022%. LoTI's ticket, at over 93%, is over 10% better. We don't claim LoTI numbers are optimal, but we expect them to be significantly better than average. While it's possible that a randomly generated ticket could outperform LoTI's, it's extremely unlikely. But if you are that lucky, you can test it using LoTI to verify your results.
